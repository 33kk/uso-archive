### Description
You can change the orange accents (e.g., the duck's round frame) in <a href="https://duckduckgo.com/"><b>DuckDuckGo</b></a>'s page to a different color (e.g., green or black) to match your preferences with this style.  The style also gives you the choice of a black duck instead of a white one.  Changing the page background color is an option in <b>DuckDuckGo</b>.
I created the style to match my <a href="https://userstyles.org/styles/113931/duckduckgo-multi-columns-custom"><b><i>DuckDuckGo - Multi-Columns Custom</i></b></a> style, featured in some screenshots. <br>
<small>(At the moment this style is only effective with <b>Firefox</b>.)</small>
<br>

### Update notes
This style re-colors <b>DuckDuckGo</b>'s logo and other branded elements in the page (three options leave the logo unaltered!).  It does not replace any artwork, nor it changes the page design in any other way.  If the layout of the search results in some screenshots differs form the usual layout, it is because of having <b><i>DuckDuckGo - Multi-Columns Custom</i></b> style enabled in those samples.

The style changes the color of the duck's disk, the page top border, the search button, the tab titles for the <i>Instant Answers</i>, the scroll buttons for those answers, the rating stars, and the <i>Official Site</i> badge.  All those are changed from orange (or green in the case of the search button) to whichever color you choose (the <i>White over Orange</i> option makes the search button orange).  The <i>Plain White</i> option sets <b>DuckDuckGo</b>'s unaltered logo with clear background but it also makes some of those changes that <b>DuckDuckGo</b> alone does not; and the <i>Plain Black</i> option is its complement.  The options for <i>White Duck Only</i> and <i>Black Duck Only</i> do not change anything but <b>DuckDuckGo</b>'s logo.

This style will not change the page background color.  This can be achieved easily through <b>DuckDuckGo</b>'s <a href="https://duckduckgo.com/settings"><i>Advanced Settings</i></a>, in the <i>Appearance</i> tab (you must allow <b>DuckDuckGo</b>'s permanent cookies for this to work properly).  Is best to do this before applying the style.  I suggest to change the background color for both the page and the header to the same color.  Note that when you change the background color, <b>DuckDuckGo</b> adjusts the color of almost everything else accordingly, including the text color.  My advice is to use a background color that is very light or very dark with low saturation; that way the text will look either white or black.  I find good results with these values:  <mark style="background-color:#181818;color:#989898;padding:0 3px 2px 2px">dark gray #181818</mark>, <mark style="background-color:#211;color:#a88;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">dark marron #221111</mark>, <mark style="background-color:#112;color:#88a;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">dark blue #111122</mark>, <mark style="background-color:#221322;color:#a8a;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">dark purple #221322</mark>, <mark style="background-color:#081b1b;color:#688;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">dark teal #081b1b</mark>, <mark style="background-color:#fcf2f2;color:#411;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">light pink #fcf2f2</mark>, <mark style="background-color:#eefdee;color:#141;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">light green #eefdee</mark>, <mark style="background-color:#afeeee;color:#144;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">pale turquoise #afeeee</mark>, and <mark style="background-color:#e9e0cf;color:#321;padding:0 3px 1px 2px">light tan #e9e0cf</mark>.  After you change the background color, you can choose a matching duck with this style.

The style options <i>Black over White</i>, <i>Black over Gold</i>, and <i>Plain White</i> should be used with a dark background, otherwise the <i>Instant Answers</i> tab titles and the stars become hard to read.  Complementary, the style options <i>White over Black</i>, and <i>Plain Black</i> should be used with a light colored background.
